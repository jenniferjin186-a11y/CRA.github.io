How to Prepare Hardware Products for CE Certification

Reduce risks, avoid failures, and pass certification faster.

CE certification is mandatory for selling electronic products in Europe. Many products fail due to poor preparation, leading to delays and extra cost.

In this guide, we walk through key steps to prepare your hardware for CE certification successfully.

Many certification failures are caused by design and EMC issues. Understanding why prototypes fail EMC testing is critical.

EMC pre-compliance testing setup in lab

1. Understand CE Certification Requirements

CE certification involves multiple directives: EMC, LVD (Low Voltage), and RED (Radio Equipment Directive, if applicable).

2. Design for EMC from the Beginning

Poor EMC design is the most common reason for failure.

EMC testing is one of the most important steps in certification. Learn about EMC testing cost and planning.

PCB layout designed for EMI reduction

  • Proper grounding and shielding
  • Optimized PCB layout
  • Filtering and decoupling

3. Perform Pre-Compliance Testing

Test early in-house to reduce risks and cost.

Pre-compliance EMC test in lab

4. Verify Structural and Safety Design

  • Insulation distances
  • Material flammability
  • Enclosure protection

5. Prepare Documentation

  • Technical file
  • Test reports
  • Risk assessment

Conclusion

Integrating EMC design, pre-compliance testing, and structural validation significantly improves CE certification success rate.
